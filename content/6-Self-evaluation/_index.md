During my internship at **FCAJ Cloud Journey** from **May 4, 2026** to **July 10, 2026**, I progressed from studying AWS foundation workshops to deploying a real full-stack project. What mattered most to me was not only learning individual services, but also understanding how to connect them into a working system that could be deployed, secured, documented, and presented clearly.

### What I did well

- I built a solid understanding of core AWS services such as IAM, VPC, EC2, RDS, S3, Cognito, API Gateway, and Amplify.
- I maintained consistent progress throughout both the learning and project-delivery phases.
- I became more independent in troubleshooting by reading logs, comparing local and cloud behavior, and testing multiple solutions before escalating.
- I completed both the technical implementation and the supporting documentation, which improved my discipline and technical writing.

### Self-rating by competency

| Competency | Self-rating | Brief note |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Technical foundation | Good | I can explain the role of the major AWS services used in the project |
| Learning speed | Good | I can study a workshop and apply the relevant ideas quickly |
| Proactiveness | Fairly good | I work independently, though I still need more confidence when choosing the best solution |
| Communication | Fairly good | I can report progress clearly, but I want to speak up more in technical discussions |
| Documentation | Good | I completed worklogs, workshop notes, and reflective sections in a structured way |
| Problem-solving | Fairly good | I improved significantly while handling deployment, API Gateway, and Cognito issues |

### What improved the most

- I moved beyond following step-by-step instructions and started understanding the purpose of each configuration.
- I became more comfortable with debugging across multiple layers: frontend, backend, infrastructure, and authentication.
- I gained a more practical view of software delivery, where coding is only one part of the full workflow.

### What I still need to improve

- I want to become more confident when presenting and defending technical ideas in front of teammates.
- I need to improve the speed of reading logs and narrowing down root causes.
- I want deeper hands-on experience with CI/CD, monitoring, and production best practices.

### Overall reflection

Overall, this internship helped me grow clearly in both technical ability and working attitude. I may not be the fastest person in every task, but I am persistent, willing to learn, and able to turn what I learn into a usable product and a complete report.
